Unbiased and Biased Estimators

Posted on 2016-05-13   |   In Statistics   |  

Problem

If a sample $ X_1,X_2 ,…, X_n $ has unknown finite variance $ \sigma^2 $, then, what is the bias between sample variance and true variance?

Analysis

Sample Variance Definition:
$$\begin{align} S^2=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

Put above equation aside, consider the identity:
$$\begin{align} \sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\mu)^2 & {=} \sum_{i=1}^{n}((X_i-\overline{X})+(\overline{X}-\mu))^{2} \nonumber\\ & {=} \sum_{i=1}^{n}((X_i-\overline{X})^2+2\sum_{i=1}^{n}((X_i-\overline{X})(\overline{X}-\mu))+(\overline{X}-\mu))^2 \nonumber\\ & {=} \sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2+\sum_{i=1}^{n}(\overline{X}-\mu)^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

So,

$$\begin{align} \sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2{=}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\mu)^2-\sum_{i=1}^{n}(\overline{X}-\mu)^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

From this and sample variance definition, we can derive that:
$$\begin{align} {E(S^2)} & {=} {E}{[}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\mu)^2-(\overline{X}-\mu)^2{]} \nonumber\\ & {=} \frac{1}{n}n\sigma^2-\frac{1}{n}\sigma^2 \nonumber\\ & {=} \frac{n-1}{n}\sigma^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

From above, we find that $ S^2 $ is a biased estimator for $ \sigma^2 $.
That is,

$$\begin{align} S_{biased}^{2}=S^2=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

In addition,
$$ E[\frac{n}{n-1}S^2]{=}\sigma^2 $$

As a result,

$$\begin{align} S_{unbiased}^{2}{=}\frac{n}{n-1}S^2{=}\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\overline{X})^2 \nonumber \end{align}$$

Conclusion

$$\begin{align} S_{biased}^{2}{=}\frac{n-1}{n}S_{unbiased}^{2} \nonumber \end{align}$$
